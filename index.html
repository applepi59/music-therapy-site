Music Therapy & Academic Stress

The Need Purpose Methods Results Activity Who Am I Resources Playlists

The Need

Academic pressure among high school and university students in Kazakhstan has significantly increased due to exams, competition, and post-pandemic recovery. Many students experience symptoms of stress, anxiety, and burnout. Addressing these issues with accessible, non-invasive strategies such as music therapy could bring both emotional relief and performance benefits.

The Purpose

This campaign aims to raise awareness about how music can support mental wellness and reduce exam-related anxiety. It promotes student-designed playlists, genre recommendations, and the use of music as a personal therapy tool. The project is research-backed and involves data collected from real students in Atyrau.

Methods

The methodology used was purposive sampling and survey-based data collection via Microsoft Forms. Students shared their music habits and experiences through structured and open-ended questions. Over 100 responses were analyzed both quantitatively and qualitatively.

Results & Analysis

The charts illustrate that most students prefer pop, classical, or lo-fi music to cope with stress. However, effectiveness also depends on personal preference and listening context. Some students reported music as a distraction, while others felt it significantly improved their emotional balance.

Sample Activity

Students were encouraged to create their own playlists or try pre-made relaxing music while studying. A sample weekly playlist is available for use, including genres mentioned by respondents as most helpful.
